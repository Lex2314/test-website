Recipes

I've mastered a handful of recipes. Some of these recipes can be made much healthier by substituing just a few ingredients, or just reducing the amount of butter/oil/processed carbs. Here are some of those recipes.

Veggie Burgers

The great thing about veggie burgers is that you can do just about anything to them. You can soak and boil dried beans to have complete control over the texture. You can caramelize onions to add to the mix. You can add whichever seasonings you like. After you put everything in the food processor, you can add whole ingredients (like chickpeas) to add texture. They're truly great. Here is the more in depth way I like to make veggie burgs. Although this way takes some additional time (namely, soaking dried beans and caramelizing veggies), most of that time is spent idle.

Ingredients:

  • Black Beans (preferably dried, but canned is also fine)
  • 1 Large Onions
  • 2 Carrots
  • 2 Stalks Celery
  • Tomato Paste
  • Handful of walnuts
  • Oats
  • Lemon
  • Any spices you want -- garlic powder, onion powder, etc
  • All other burger essentials -- buns, pickles, ketchup, etc

Instructions

1. Step One: Bean Prep

If you are using dry beans, congrats. Soak your beans overnight in water -- this helps remove phytochemicals that cause bloating. Simmer for around an hour until desired consistency. If using canned beans, drain off liquid then reheat in a pot so that they will eventially mash more easily.

2. Step Two: Caramelize

Caramelize onions, carrots, and celery. Start pan on high heat, add sliced veggies, then reduce heat. The goal here is to caramelize these veggies without burning them. This will add a tremendous depth of flavor that is absolutely worth the 45 minutes to an hour it takes. Don't be afraid to add a small amount of tomato paste towards the end of the caramelization process to add a further depth of flavor. Once done, you can set both the caramelized veg and warmed beans to the side.

3. Toast Walnuts

Get your pan hot and add some oil. Add your walnuts and toss them to get them covered in oil. Shimmy pan every now and then to add some color to the walnuts without them burning. Once toasted, throw them in the food processor to grind them up. This is my favorite step -- it smells amazing.

4. Combine Ingredients

Throw everything into the food processor and run it until everything is smooth and combined. Taste the mixture and add any spices you think will be good (garlic powder, onion powder, sage, thyme, salt, and pepper are all good options). Add in some lemon juice to taste (around a quarter to a half lemon). You might want to do this stage in two batches, because it can become to run the food processor if it becomes too full. Once you have the desired taste, scoop everything into a large mixing bowl. You can add oats at this stage to help the patties stick together. Work in small amounts of oats at a time so that you don't dry out the mixture.

5. Burger Time

Grab a handful and make your patty. Veggie burgers will stay together, but do tend to fall apart more easily than ground beef, so pan frying tends to be easier. That being said, they're still great grilled. Prepare your burgers however you like, and enjoy.

Final Notes

This veggie burger mix is great for meal prep, as you can easily grab a handful and throw it on a pan. You can also experiment using this veggie mixture in tacos or whatever other creative way you see fit. The mixture will be very nutrient rich and will start to turn after a few days in the fridge, so try to use it up or freeze it within about 3 days.
